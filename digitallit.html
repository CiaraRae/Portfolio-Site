Course Reflection

As the semester began, and I considered my own digital literacy, I classified my learning as "a story of making things up as I go". While I would love to say that my methods have become more sophisticated over time, this course has shown me that when it comes to learning and adapting to new technology, trial and error is inevitable. My desire to understand everything (and by extension be good at everything), made the start of semester intimidating. Diving into new topics that I was completely unfamiliar with, from HTML & CSS to static site hosting, left me anxiously wondering if my digital literacy would advance quickly enough to keep up. However, looking back on how much we've accomplished in a few short months, it makes me proud to look back at my completed projects. Likewise, while I've discovered so many new resources (see Personal Learning Network section) this experience has also made me realize that the virtual classroom learning experience, with its supportive peer community and curated documents, is an ideal space for me when exploring complicated topics. It makes the process much more managable and keeps me accountable to continue progressing.
