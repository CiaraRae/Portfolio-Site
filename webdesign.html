Project Overview


Minnesota Austin Healey Club Website

Our project team approached the redesign of the Minnesota Austin Healey Club website with the following goals in mind:

  1. Create an accessible and easily navigable site that helps users without high digital literacy find what they need.
    Our team assumed two distinct audiences when planning the redesign of the Minnesota Austin Healey Club website: current club members and potential club members. Current club members range from casual enthusiasts to active racers. Many current club members currently own an Austin Healey, but several do not. Potential club members include those who own an Austin Healey, those who do not currently own an Austin Healey but are interested in ownership, and those who do not own an Austin Healey but are interested in Austin Healeys and British sports cars in general. Both categories skew older and tend to have below average digital literacy prompting a design high in accessibility and usability.
  2. Increase membership of the club and use the website as a resource to grow the organization.
    The Minnesota Austin Healey Club website is representative of the club itself and should serve to retain current membership, attract new membership, and highlight the many resources it offers Austin Healey enthusiasts.
  3. Create a platform to serve as a hub for social interaction and networking between people with a common interest.
    As the Minnesota Austin Healey Club site administrator role is evolving and dependent on current club membership and needs, Group Orange suggests utilizing a linked forum-type platform, such as a Facebook group or similar, that allows for social interaction and networking without requiring the site administrator to act as a forum moderator. Discussion moderation on an external platform could easily fall to a digitally savvy member(s).
